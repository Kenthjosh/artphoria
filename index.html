about me

Hi, I’m Remelyn Mongaya Estanque, a third-year BSIT student. I'm really passionate about both technology and creativity, which is why I love being a part of "The Modern Fisher," our campus organization. I’m also the Cartoon Editor, where I get to use my artistic side to bring fun and engaging stories to life. Balancing school and being involved in campus activities is important to me, and I’m always looking for ways to grow and make a positive impact on my college community.

Ever since I was a child, my passion for drawing has been deeply rooted in my love for anime. Watching captivating stories filled with vibrant characters inspired me to pick up a pencil and bring my imagination to life. I spent countless hours in my room sketching my favorite characters, immersing myself in the creative process. Over time, I began to recognize my potential, and my determination to improve led me to participate in various art competitions. Winning many of these contests from elementary through senior high school fueled my passion even more and solidified my dream of sharing stories through art.

Now, as a third-year college student, I have come a long way from the kid who spent days perfecting anime sketches. When I first heard about the screening for The Modern Fisher, our campus publication, I immediately saw it as a chance to turn my passion into a meaningful contribution. I was thrilled to be accepted as a cartoonist and proudly served as Chief Cartoonist during my first and second years. Today, that same kid who once drew anime characters is the Cartoon Editor of The Modern Fisher, where I contribute engaging, creative stories that resonate with our readers.
